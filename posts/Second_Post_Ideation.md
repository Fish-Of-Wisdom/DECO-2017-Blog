# Personal Ideation

Before coming together to decide on a concept, I did some initial ideation. Most of the ideas were more broad umbrellas - a general understanding of the groups that may find the most use out of the social forum posting the Design Brief is asking for. 

### Fandoms (games, books, media)
- easy to tailor to a specific franchise
- benefit most from informative based, static media (walkthroughs, lore explanations)
- potential lack of longevity due to the finite amount of content

### Skincare and/or Beauty
- potentially harder to really specifically tailor as by nature it's a much broader category
- very active community and high strength of longevity - with new products and new people always looking, there is always opportunity for socialisation
- opportunity for categorical distinctions

### Coding and similar
- good potential, because similar things such as Stack Overflow already exist !
- some kind of technology help guide may be a potential
- allow people to post images and/or text to seek help from more experienced users
- requires attracting experienced people


All of these communities, to a certain extent, already utilise social communication platforms, the most common of which being Reddit, Discord, and (in the case of coding) Stack Overflow. Communities that have a very strong identity are the best demographics for the platform we are building, as they allow for a much more dyanamic, specific experience, and are the most likely to seek out a separate space specific for sharing with *their* community members. 

# Settling On A Concept

My group member Molly had an idea about making a platform for international students studying at USYD - as an exchange student herself, she has had difficulty finding other people from her country also studying at USYD, and often only runs in to them on accident. 

We felt this concept tied very well into the design brief and it's requirements, especially in consideration to unique user experiences, and presents an opportunity to solve a problem felt locally by USYD students. 

- concept based on social interaction within a specific community (international/exchange students)
- allows for tailoring to USYD branding, and to specific countries (visually)
- inherently based on specific users, with a communal anchor point 
- stretch goal opportunities (e.g potentially scraping the USYD website to automatically populate degree fields)
