# Personal Ideation

Before coming together to decide on a concept, I did some initial ideation. Most of the ideas were more broad umbrellas - a general understanding of the groups that may find the most use out of the social forum posting the Design Brief is asking for. 

### Fandoms (games, books, media) 
- easy to <span class="mint">tailor </span> to a <span class="mint">specific </span>franchise
- benefit most from informative based, <span class="blush">static media </span>(walkthroughs, lore explanations)
- potential <span class="blush">lack of longevity </span> due to the <span class="blush"> finite </span> amount of content

### Skincare and/or Beauty
- potentially <span class="blush">harder </span> to really <span class="blush">specifically tailor </span> as by nature it's a much broader category
- very <span class="mint">active community </span> and high <span class="mint"> strength of longevity </span>- with new products and new people always looking, there is always <span class="mint">opportunity </span>for socialisation 
- opportunity for categorical<span class="mint"> distinctions </span>

### Coding and similar
- good <span class="mint">potential </span>, because similar things such as Stack Overflow <span class="blush">already exist </span> !
- some kind of technology help guide may be a potential
- allow people to post images and/or text to seek help from more experienced users
- requires<span class="blush"> attracting experienced people </span>


All of these communities, to a certain extent, already <span class="mint"> utilise social communication platforms </span>, the most common of which being Reddit, Discord, and (in the case of coding) Stack Overflow. Communities that have a very<span class="mint"> strong identity </span> are the best demographics for the platform we are building, as they allow for a much more <span class="mint">dyanamic, specific experience, </span> and are the most likely to seek out a <span class="mint">separate space </span> specific for sharing with *their* community members. 

# Settling On A Concept

My group member Molly had an idea about making a platform for <span class="orange">international students </span> studying at USYD - as an exchange student herself, she has had difficulty finding other people from her country also studying at USYD, and often only runs in to them on accident. 

We felt this concept tied very well into the<span class="teal"> design brief </span> and it's requirements, especially in consideration to<span class="teal"> unique user experiences, </span> and presents an opportunity to solve a problem felt locally by USYD students. 

- concept based on <span class="mint">social interaction </span> within a specific community (international/exchange students)
- allows for <span class="mint">tailoring </span> to USYD branding, and to specific countries (visually)
- inherently based on specific users, with a <span class="mint">communal </span>anchor point 
- stretch goal opportunities (e.g potentially scraping the USYD website to automatically populate degree fields)
