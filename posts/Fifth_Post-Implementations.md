# Implementation

# User Testing Plan

We decided our<span class="mauve"> user testing</span> would be completed very close to the <span class="mauve">completion</span> of our project, instead of earlier in the process - such as with<span class="mauve"> wireframes</span> or <span class="mauve">static pages</span>. 

This was chosen because we wanted our testing to be <span class="mauve">evaluative </span>over <span class="mauve">feasible</span>: we aren't looking for if our idea was one worth exploring, but instead how <span class="mauve">well</span> our idea was <span class="mauve">executed</span>. 

What this also enables for us is to have <span class="mauve">external opinions</span>, <span class="mauve">findings</span> and <span class="mauve">learnings</span> for our reflection! It also <span class="mauve">opens discussions</span> for changes we would, or would not make, based on that feedback, without feeling <span class="mauve">pressured</span> to quickly make those changes in our prototype/demo. There is more time to, well, <span class="mauve">reflect</span>, on that feedback, decide what it means for the project, and if we would <span class="mauve">like </span>to implement it and why. 

Our plan for user testing would involve <span class="mauve">booting up</span> our project, and having people <span class="mauve">complete tasks </span>of our choosing, with a <span class="mauve">think-aloud</span> whilst they were going through. This would be followed by a SUS <span class="mauve">(System Usability Scale) </span>survey and accompanying think aloud about their scoring options.

These methods create a <span class="mauve">comprehensive qualitative</span> and <span class="mauve">quantitative</span> results pool, with a fairly <span class="mauve">cheap cost</span> of time (usually around 10-20 minutes per participant). They are also methods that <span class="mauve">fit well </span>with <span class="mauve">evaluative</span> testing of a more final stage design, and we can reflect <span class="mauve">different groups</span> of people, their <span class="mauve">interests </span>and how they <span class="mauve">engage</span> with digital products even through a <span class="mauve">small </span>sample size.
