# Wireframing


I decided to <span class="teal"> visualise </span> potential layouts and core functionalities of our design solution by creating <span class="teal">wireframes </span>. After my groupmate sent through a rough guide of a home page layout, 

<img class="toSize" src="assets/img/initial.jpg">

I created a more <span class="teal">defined wireframe </span> using Figma, with the aid of the Blocks - Wireframe plugin to speed up the design workflow. The wireframes have been created for a <span class="teal">desktop view </span>, as most students primary device for university activities is a laptop/desktop computer. After this, my groupmate and I held a meeting, wherein we discuseed changes and further refined our ideas of layout and necessary features. 

## Concept Frames and Changes

### Home Screen

<img class="toSize" src="assets/img/home-page.png">

This was a very<span class="teal"> direct </span> translation of the skeleton guide. It features a top nav-bar with course and country specification (which would alter the content appearing, and potentially as a<span class="teal"> stretch goal </span> or next steps would allow for automatic translation between languages), and a 'home' discussion section that features a <span class="teal">main discussion </span>, likely to be a general discussion regarding USYD as a whole (announcements, general events, building notifications as examples). 

This would then be followed by other discussions, potentially ones that are followed or deemed relevant to the user, which could then be scrolled through.



After discussion, the <span class="teal">biggest change </span> we decided to implement was an additional sidebar (which will be pictured further) to the right of the discussion section, to allow for a<span class="teal"> quick </span> area to <span class="teal">access </span> other discussions they follow or are active in. 

Another change was to add an opportunity for 'quick replying' to posts and other user replies, which sets up <span class="teal">convenience </span> and more strongly encourages <span class="teal">communication </span>.

The last change was to add a 'post' button to <span class="teal">visibly allow </span> for post creation on the home page itself.

### General Discussion Area
<img class="toSize" src="assets/img/general-post.png">

This wireframe depicts the view of the <span class="teal">general discussions area </span> - where the discussion cards are able to be scrolled through. The right-sidebar for followed, favorited, or recommended discussions is also on this page, allowing for more <span class="teal">customisation </span> and <span class="teal">organisation </span> for users.

### Creating A Post
<img class="toSize" src="assets/img/post-create.png">

This wireframe is a view of the <span class="teal">post creation process </span>, with relevant text fields and buttons, allowing users to create a new post <span class="teal">within a discussion </span>. It is not intended to be used for replying to others, as that will be handled by the <span class="teal">'quick reply'</span>. 

This may also serve as the<span class="teal"> blueprint </span>, or at least the starting point, of a <span class="teal">discussion creation view </span>, which will handle the creation of the over arching discussion itself (similar to Reddit - this would be the subreddit creation, and the above would be the OP post being created for others to reply to.)

### Messaging
<img class="toSize" src="assets/img/message.png">

This view may fall <span class="teal">out of scope </span> for this project, but presents an idea of how a <span class="teal">messaging function </span> would be laid out and function. It is fairly standard, with a left side-bar for switching conversations, a header with the contactee's details, and a large chat log.
