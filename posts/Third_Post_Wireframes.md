# Wireframing


I decided to visualise potential layouts and core functionalities of our design solution by creating wireframes. After my groupmate sent through a rough guide of a home page layout, 

{ IMAGE }

I created a more defined wireframe using Figma, with the aid of the Blocks - Wireframe plugin to speed up the design workflow. The wireframes have been created for a desktop view, as most students primary device for university activities is a laptop/desktop computer. After this, my groupmate and I held a meeting, wherein we discuseed changes and further refined our ideas of layout and necessary features. 

## Concept Frames and Changes

### Home Screen
{IMAGE 1: HOMESCREEN}

This was a very direct translation of the skeleton guide. It features a top nav-bar with course and country specification (which would alter the content appearing, and potentially as a stretch goal or next steps would allow for automatic translation between languages), and a 'home' discussion section that features a main discussion, likely to be a general discussion regarding USYD as a whole (announcements, general events, building notifications as examples). 

This would then be followed by other discussions, potentially ones that are followed or deemed relevant to the user, which could then be scrolled through.

{IMAGE 2: ANNOTATED HOMESCREEN}

After discussion, the biggest change we decided to implement was an additional sidebar (which will be pictured further) to the right of the discussion section, to allow for a quick area to access other discussions they follow or are active in. 

Another change was to add an opportunity for 'quick replying' to posts and other user replies, which sets up convenience and more strongly encourages communication.

The last change was to add a 'post' button to visibly allow for post creation on the home page itself.

### General Discussion Area
{IMAGE 3: GENERAL POST}

This wireframe depicts the view of the general discussions area - where the discussion cards are able to be scrolled through. The right-sidebar for followed, favorited, or recommended discussions is also on this page, allowing for more customisation and organisation for users. 

{potential for annotated general post although I believe that the annotations are mostly references to other frames}

### Creating A Post
{IMAGE 4: POST CREATION}

This wireframe is a view of the post creation process, with relevant text fields and buttons, allowing users to create a new post within a discussion. It is not intended to be used for replying to others, as that will be handled by the 'quick reply'. 

This may also serve as the blueprint, or at least the starting point, of a discussion creation view, which will handle the creation of the over arching discussion itself (similar to Reddit - this would be the subreddit creation, and the above would be the OP post being created for others to reply to.)

### Messaging
{IMAGE 5: MESSAGING}

This view may fall out of scope for this project, but presents an idea of how a messaging function would be laid out and function. It is fairly standard, with a left side-bar for switching conversations, a header with the contactee's details, and a large chat log.
